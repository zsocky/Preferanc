Preferánsz: Game Rules

The following version of the game is reported from the area of the Former Yugoslavia. Hungarian Preferánsz is very similar.

Preferánsz is played by three to four active players with a Hungarian 32-card deck. Aces rank high and tens rank in their natural position between jacks and nines. As happens with many three-player trick-taking games, the game is frequently played by four players using the convention that in each hand the dealer pauses. Each active player receives 10 cards in batches of 2. The remaining 2 cards form a talon that will be used by the declarer to improve his or her hand. The deal typically follows the scheme: 5-5-5-talon(2)-5-5-5.

Ofertele numerice sunt cele prezentate în tabel. Există un contract de mână corespunzător pentru fiecare contract normal. Dacă mai mulți jucători licitează mână, contractul cel mai mare are prioritate ca și în cazul ofertelor fără mână. În cazul contractelor de mână, talonul este pus deoparte. În caz contrar, declarantul îl expune apărătorilor, apoi ia cele două cărți și aruncă oricare 2 cărți pentru a ajunge la o mână de 10 cărți. Declarantul anunță orice contract a cărui valoare este cel puțin egală cu cea a ofertei.

Trick-play differs from Whist in that there is an obligation to trump. The eldest hand leads to the first trick. Players must follow suit if possible, else trump if possible. The trick is won by the player who played the highest trump or the highest card of the suit led. The winner of a trick leads to the next trick.
